Description

Unlike plasmid, GFP has two sticky ends on the left and right sides, because the insertion occurs in the middle of a plasmid gap (see points 2 and 3 in the figure). Now, you need to obtain GFP with sticky ends. For GFP, the best restriction site is CTGCAG from the left and GTGCAC from the right.

To visualize the process of restriction, you can watch the video on YouTube by Nicole Lantz.

Your may work as follows:

  1. In GFP original sequence, find the first entrance of the CTGCAG from the left and the first entrance of the GTGCAC site from the right.
  2. Make a cut in the CTGCAG site between 1st and 2nd nucleotides (C and T). Also make a cut in the GTGCAC site between 1st and 2nd nucleotides (G and T).
  3. Create a complementary GFP strand and complementary strands for both restriction sites based on your knowlegde from the stage 1.
  4. In GFP complementary sequence, find the first entrance of the site complementary to the CTGCAG site from the left. Cut it between 5th and 6th nucleotides (T and C).
  5. In the same GFP strand, find the first entrance of the site complementary to the GTGCAC site from the right. Cut it between 5th and 6th nucleotides (T and G).

Another way to obtain GFP cuts is presented in Objectives. You may use either method at your discretion.

Objectives
  1. Input the original GFP gene sequence and restriction sites from the console;
  2. To automate the action of getting the sticky ends in the GFP plasmid, we recommend implementing a function that will (or you can use another way from the Description part):
    • Create complementary sequences for the GFP gene and for the restriction sites;
    • Find the restriction site CTGCAG at the beginning of the sequence, and cut both strands: the original one between 1st and 2nd nucleotides, and the complementary one between 5th and 6th nucleotides. As a result of this step, we will get a protruding sticky end on the original GFP chain;
    • Find the GTGCAC restriction site at the end of the sequence, and cut through it similarly to the previous point;
    • Return the resulting GFP chains.

As a result, print the double-stranded GFP sequence with sticky ends. The original and complementary strands should be separated by a newline character (\n).

Example

The greater-than symbol followed by a space (> ) represents the console input.

Example 1: input of the original strand and the restriction sites

> CTGCAG TTGCAC
