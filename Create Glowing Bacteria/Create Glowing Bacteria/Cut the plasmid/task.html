Description

In the previous stage, we obtained a double-strand bacterial plasmid. Now, you need to cut it at a certain place to make a gap. This particular place is called a restriction site. In nature, the cutting is provided by special enzymes called restrictases. They are like scissors. After cutting, sticky ends are formed. To get the sticky ends means to make a "diagonal" cut at the restriction site.

Objectives

Use the first fragment below. Cut it at the site CTGCAG to make a gap between C and T . Then use the second fragment (complementary strand). Create the complementaty site for CTGCAG (A <-> T, C <-> G). Find complementary site in the second fragment. Cut the site between last two nucleotides (T and C ). So, your sequence for cutting is as follows:


original and complementary strands (fragments) are separated by a space

In your answer, include both fragments and mark the place of the cut with a single space. Place each strand (fragment) on a separate line. Use capital letters only.
